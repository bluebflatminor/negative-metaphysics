## What this is

There is a genre of essay claiming that quantum mechanics vindicated Eastern metaphysics — that Bell's theorem confirms Nāgārjuna, that entanglement proves the Daoists right. This guide locates the exact seam where that inference fails, and hands the reader a portable tool for finding it themselves.

The core distinction: a physical theorem can **select** a worldview (hand you a positive picture of what there is) or **subtract** one (foreclose a combination of assumptions and fall silent). Bell and Kochen–Specker do the second job. The genre needs them to have done the first. Everything in the guide follows from that.

Section 10 turns the same tool on a genre that didn't exist when the guide began: essays reading machine consciousness (in either direction — "waking up" / "just autocomplete") into the July 2026 Anthropic global-workspace interpretability finding. Same seam, new subject.

## Method

- **Falsification-first.** For every claim: what would show it false? If nothing could, it's resonance dressed as evidence — including on this page.
- **DOI gate.** Every source must resolve to the cited title, authors, and venue before deployment.
- **Resolution ≠ content (new in v0.7).** A citation can resolve perfectly while the sentence built on it mischaracterizes the source. Content claims require a quote-level anchor or an explicit `pending` flag. The working notes distinguish the two.
- **Recorded debts.** Unverified claims are demoted and flagged in-text, not silently retained. Pending items block v1.0.
